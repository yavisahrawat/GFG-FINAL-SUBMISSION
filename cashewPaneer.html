Cashew Paneer

Ingredients

  • 200 grams paneer, cubed
  • 1 cup cashews, soaked in water for at least 2 hours
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnishing
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil

Instructions to follow

  • Grind the soaked cashews into a smooth paste using a blender or food processor. Set aside.
  • Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  • Add the finely chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.
  • Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for a minute or until the raw smell disappears.
  • Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn mushy and the oil starts to separate.
  • Add red chili powder, turmeric powder, and coriander powder. Mix well and cook for a couple of minutes.
  • Add the cashew paste and mix thoroughly, letting it cook for a few minutes.
  • Add the cubed paneer and gently mix it with the cashew gravy, ensuring the paneer is well coated.
  • Pour in the heavy cream and stir gently to incorporate.
  • Add garam masala and salt according to your taste. Stir and let it cook for a few more minutes.
  • Once the paneer is cooked and the gravy reaches your desired consistency, turn off the heat.
  • Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot with naan, roti, or rice.
