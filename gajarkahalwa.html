Gajar Ka Halwa

Ingredients

  • 4 cups grated carrots
  • 3 cups whole milk
  • 1/2 cup sugar (adjust according to your taste)
  • 4 tablespoons ghee (clarified butter)
  • 1+1/2 strong brewed cup of coffee.
  • 1/4 cup mixed nuts (almonds, cashews, and raisins), chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • A pinch of saffron strands (optional)
  • A handful of chopped pistachios for garnishing

Instructions to follow

  • Heat ghee in a heavy-bottomed pan or a non-stick pan on medium heat.
  • Add the grated carrots and sauté for 5-7 minutes until they turn soft and the raw smell disappears.
  • Pour in the milk and let the carrots cook in the milk. Stir occasionally to prevent the milk from sticking to the bottom of the pan.
  • Cook the carrots in the milk until the milk reduces and thickens, and the carrots become soft and cooked. This might take around 20-25 minutes.
  • Add sugar, chopped nuts, cardamom powder, and saffron strands (if using). Mix well and cook for another 5-10 minutes until the mixture thickens further and attains a halwa-like consistency.
  • Keep stirring intermittently to avoid sticking and to ensure even cooking.
  • Once the mixture reaches a thick consistency and the ghee starts to separate from the mixture, turn off the heat.
  • Garnish with chopped pistachios.
  • Serve the gajar ka halwa warm or chilled, according to your preference.
